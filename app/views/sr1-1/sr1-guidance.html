Changes to the Special Rules process in September 2022

The Special Rules allow people nearing the end of life to:

  • get faster, easier access to some benefits
  • get higher payments for some benefits
  • avoid a medical assessment

These rules were previously called the Special Rules for Terminal Illness (SRTI) and applied to people who had 6 months or less to live. They have been renamed the Special Rules for End of Life (SREL) and apply to people who have 12 months or less to live.

In September 2022, the DWP widened the eligibility criteria of the Special Rules to allow individuals who are likely to have less than 12 months to live to make fast-tracked claims for:

  • Universal Credit (UC)
  • Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
  • Attendance Allowance (AA)
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

This change has allowed the DWP to align with the current NHS/GMC definition of ‘end of life’ which states that ‘patients are approaching the end of life when they are likely to die within the next 12 months.’

Clinicians are encouraged to identify patients who have reached this stage of their life and use a holistic approach to consider what support their patient may require.

The SR1 form

Clinicians can be asked to provide medical evidence on an SR1 form to support a benefit claim made under the Special Rules.

A clinician may be a:

  • GP
  • hospital consultant
  • speciality doctor
  • hospice doctor
  • senior nurse (employed at Band 7 or 8, who may be working as a clinical nurse specialist, advanced nurse practitioner or similar)

Clinicians should complete an SR1 form promptly if they believe that their patient:

  • has a progressive disease and
  • you would not be surprised if they were to live for less than 12 months

There are no negative consequences if a patient who claims under the Special Rules lives longer than expected.

Using the SR1 form to provide medical evidence

It’s important that you know about your patient’s condition.

The SR1 form asks for details of:

  • diagnosis/diagnoses and other relevant conditions
  • whether the patient is aware of their diagnosis and prognosis
  • clinical features that indicate a severe progressive condition (for example, investigation results, staging if appropriate etc.)
  • information about treatment that has been received, is ongoing or planned

You do not need to give a specific prognosis. Please use language that you would normally use when communicating with other clinicians.

Claims on behalf of patients

  • UC and ESA: only an appointee or a person who has power of attorney can make a third party claim on the patient’s behalf
  • DLA, PIP and AA: any person representing the patient can make a third party claim on their behalf, even if the patient is unaware that a claim is being made

Claiming a fee

If you are a GP or GMC registered consultant, you can claim a fee for completing an SR1 form by completing a separate fee form.

Returning the SR1 forms

You can return the SR1 form through the NHS Spine Portal, by email or by post.

Through the NHS Spine portal

This service is available from the NHS Portal on your desktop. You’ll need an NHS smartcard to access the service. Once you have accessed the NHS Portal, click on ‘Launch Digital SR1 Service’ and complete the SR1 form online.

Once you have submitted the SR1 form online, you can download a copy of the fee form. This can be returned by post to the address on the form.

By email

Complete an electronic version of the SR1 form and the fee form and email it to form.e-SR1@dwp.gov.uk using a secure email address.

Secure email addresses end in @nhs.net, @nhs.uk, @wales.nhs.uk, @nhs.scot and @[x].gov.uk.

Do not encrypt any part of the email. Any email that does not come from a secure email account will be automatically deleted.

No signature is required on either form for this method of return.

To get a copy of the electronic version of the SR1 form and fee form, please send an email from your secure email address to form.e-SR1@dwp.gov.uk with details of:

  • your secure email address or addresses
  • your surgery/hospital/office postal address and telephone number
  • a list of medical professionals using the same secure email address who complete the SR1 form and/or the fee form
  • whether you need the SR1 form and the fee form, or the SR1 form only

By post

If you’ve completed a paper copy of the SR1 form or fee form, please sign it. Only original signed copies of these forms will be accepted by post.

There are 2 options for returning the forms to us:

  • give the SR1 form to the person who requested it and return the fee form separately to us, or
  • post the completed SR1 form and fee form directly to: [address]

When returning SR1 forms, please use a separate envelope for each patient. Please do not staple documents together.
