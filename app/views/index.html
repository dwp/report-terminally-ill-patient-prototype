(Archived) Ds1500 Prototypes for Design Team for User Testing

This section is for the Design Team to test new features with users. This section is not for the Development Team. The Development Team should refer to Prototype for Developers section.

Prototype v1 is designed with several questions per page
Prototype v2 is designed with 1 question per page
Prototype v3 is designed with all questions on one single page. Please note that this prototype does not have check your answers functionality due to fear of duplication
Prototype v4 is designed with all questions on one single page. Since a check your answers functionality is not recommended to avoid duplication, Prototype v4 tests a YES/NO Radio component at the end of the form stating: have you checked all the information you have entered for errors?
Prototype v5 is designed with all questions on one single page. Further user research has indicated that users may actually want a check your answers page as it is a chance to "preview" the application before its submitted. Therefore this prototype contains "check your answers" functionality
Prototype v6 experiments with some new minor designs and components to aid the flow of the service, with the aim of A/B Testing them.
Prototype v7 experiments with builds from a team workshop on Friday 8th Jan.
Prototype v8 is implemented with builds from a series of UR sessions with medical professionals starting 2nd February 2021.
Prototype v9 is implemented with builds from a series of UR sessions and accessibility sessions with medical professionals starting in late February 2021.
Prototypes v10a, v10b and v10c test having DS1500 and Claim Fee Form imagery on the confirmation page. v10a (imagery), v10b (no imagery) and v10c (no imagery, with green buttons).
Prototypes v11a, v11b and v11c focus on testing the security warning messages at the top of the pages. v11a contains the messages (warning component), v11b removes the messages and v11c tests the Notification Banner component on the confirmation page.
Prototype 12 focuses re-introducing the one-question per page format. It copies Prototype v11b (but in a one-question per page format).
It was brought to our attention that the GDS experimental Accordion component , despite testing very well, has an accessibility issue, therefore may need temporary removal from the service. Therefore the following prototypes look to explore at alternative methods of displaying the content that was meant for the accordion. Currently as it stands, the accordion's section headings can be mistaken for links, but are treated as buttons. This fails WCAG 2.1 success criterion 1.3.1 Info and Relationships. Prototype v13a implements Contents functionality similar to the (COVID-19) Coronavirus restrictions: what you can and cannot do guidance page. Prototype 13b incorporates the Content in a separate guidance page.

In addition, both prototypes have incorporated a 'Specialist Nurse' Radio button in Details section of the form as they are a high proportion of users of the DS1500 form. Data analysis on this Radio button is pending.

Prototype 14 introduces the accessbile link for additional guidance on the start page. It also tests splitting out the confirmation screen into separate screens, download and confirmation.
