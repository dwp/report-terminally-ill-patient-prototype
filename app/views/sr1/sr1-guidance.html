Changes to the Special Rules process in 2022

The Special Rules criteria are changing from applying to people with less than 6 months to live to applying to people with less than 12 months to live.

Due to parliamentary constraints, the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) can only change the rules for 2 out of 5 benefits from April 2022.

These are Universal Credit (UC) and New Style Employment Support (ESA).

There will be:

  • criteria for 2 sets of special rules to consider
  • a new medical evidence form SR1, which will operate alongside the form DS1500

This is a transitional period until the new rules are extended to all 5 benefits.

Find out more about the changes to the Special Rules and completing the forms (opens in a new tab).

When to complete a DS1500

You should complete the DS1500 form promptly if you believe that your patient:

  • has a progressive disease, and
  • as a consequence of that disease, you would not be surprised if your patient were to die within 6 months

When you provide a DS1500 to DWP, it will be shared across all necessary benefit platforms. You do not need to complete a SR1 form as well.

When to complete an SR1

You should complete the SR1 form promptly if you believe that your patient:

  • has a progressive disease, and
  • as a consequence of that disease you would not be surprised if your patient were to die within 12 months

If you think that your patient has a shorter prognosis, for example, less than 6 months to live, complete a DS1500 instead.

About your patient

Your patient’s prognosis

It is understood that determining life expectancy in these circumstances is not an exact science. You will not face any negative consequences from the factual information you supply, for example if your patient lives longer than expected.

Patients who may not know the true nature of their illness

When a patient asks you to complete a DS1500 or SR1 form, do not assume that they understand the special rules criteria or that these criteria may apply to them. It may be an opportunity to initiate a conversation about advance care planning.

Who should complete the SR1 or DS1500 form

This form can be completed by a registered clinician such as:

  • a GP
  • a hospital doctor
  • a registered nurse such as a Macmillian nurse, advanced nurse practitioner, clinical specialist nurse or practice nurse

Third party claims

For Universal Credit and New Style Employment Support Allowance, only an appointee or a person who has power of attorney can make a third party claim on the patient’s behalf.

For Disability Living Allowance, Attendance Allowance and Personal Independence Payment, any person representing the patient can make a third party claim on their behalf, even if the patient is unaware that a claim is being made. In this circumstance the form should be issued to the person that requested it.

Benefits that can be claimed under special rules

If your patient meets the 6-month special rules criteria, they can make claims for the following benefits:

  • Universal Credit
  • New Style Employment and Support Allowance
  • Personal Independence Payment
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Attendance Allowance

If your patient meets the new 12-month special rule criteria they can make claims for:

  • Universal Credit
  • New Style Employment and Support Allowance

If your patient meets the Special Rules criteria, their benefit claim will be processed as a priority and they will not be required to attend an assessment.

How this form will be used by DWP

This form allows you to provide further evidence to enable us to make a decision on your patient’s claim. It is not a claim form, as you cannot claim on behalf of your patients. It will not normally be necessary for you to examine the patient. You may use your own knowledge and the patient’s records to get the information you need.

Telephone contact

As we need to deal with these claims urgently, the healthcare professional will usually contact you by telephone.

You may be contacted by a healthcare professional working for one of our clinical assessment providers if:

  • if we need clarification of some of the information on the DS1500 to help with our decision about the claim, or
  • the patient has made a claim under the Special Rules process but has not submitted a DS1500 or SR1 form and we need clinical information to support the claim

Claiming a fee

You can claim a fee if you are a GP or GMC registered consultant, but only if you have authorised the fee form by signing it.

To claim your fee, download and fill out the form at the end.

We will pay it into your bank or building society account.

For claims to New Style Employment and Support Allowance or Universal Credit send the fee form to the nearest Jobcentre Plus office (opens in a new tab).
