When you should complete the DS1500 Report

This form is called the DS1500 form and its purpose is to tell the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) about a patient who meets the 6-month special rules criteria.

You should complete this form promptly if you believe that your patient meets the 6-month special rules criteria. The criteria are:

  • they have a progressive disease and,
  • as a consequence of that disease you would not be surprised if your patient were to die within 6 months.

The 6-month special rules criteria do not only apply to patients with cancer. They also apply to severe, life limiting cardiorespiratory and neurological conditions. this list is not exhaustive.

Benefits that can be claimed under special rules

Special rules claims can be made in the following benefits:

  • Universal Credit
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Personal Independence Payment
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Attendance Allowance

If your patient meets the special rules criteria, their benefit claim will be processed as a priority and in the majority of cases they will receive the highest rate of benefit.

Who should complete the DS1500 form

You should complete this form if you would like to encourage your patient to see which benefits they may be entitled to as part of a discussion about future care planning or if requested by your patient or their representative.

This form can be completed by a registered clinician such as:

  • a GP
  • a hospital doctor
  • a registered nurse

A registered nurse needs to have acquired the expert knowledge and clinical competencies to undertake the assessment.

They may be working in a role such as an advanced nurse practitioner, a Macmillan nurse, a clinical specialist nurse or a practice nurse with expertise in the management of long term conditions.

It will not normally be necessary for you to examine the patient. You may use your own knowledge and the patient’s records to get the information you need.

When should you complete a SR1 form

A new form, the SR1, has been introduced for patients who meet the 12-month special rules criteria for Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) and Universal Credit (UC).

For further information about changes to the special rules and completing medical reports for DWP, go to www.gov.uk and search for‘DWP factual medical reports’.

Third party claims

For Disability Living Allowance, Attendance Allowance and Personal Independence Payment only, any person representing the patient can make a 3rd party claim on their behalf, even if the patient is unaware that a claim is being made. In this circumstance the form should be issued on request to the representative.

Your patient’s prognosis

Determining life expectancy in these circumstances is not an exact science. This form asks for factual information and does not require you to give a prognosis. Some patients may meet the 6-month special rules criteria whilst getting treatment.

Use language that you would normally use when communicating with other clinicians.

You will not face any negative consequences from the factual information you supply, for example if your patient lives longer than 6 months.

Please visit www.gov.uk and search for ‘DWP factual medical reports’ if you would like more information regarding:

  • clinical circumstances that might suggest a prognosis likely to meet the 6-month special rules criteria
  • links to further guidance related to end of life care

Patients who may not know the true nature of their illness

When a patient asks you for a DS1500 form, please do not assume that they understand the 6-month special rules criteria or that these criteria may apply to them.

Telephone contact

You may be contacted by a healthcare professional working for one of our clinical assessment providers if:

  • if we need clarification of some of the information on the DS1500 to help with our decision about the claim, or
  • if the patient has made a claim under the Special Rules procedures but has not submitted a DS1500 and we need clinical information to support the claim

Because we need to deal with these claims urgently, the healthcare professional will usually contact you by telephone. The purpose of the call is not to challenge the clinical judgement of clinicians.

Claiming a fee

You can claim a fee if you are a GP or GMC registered consultant, but only if you have authorised this form by signing it at the end.

To claim your fee, you will download and fill out the form at the end.

We will pay your fee into your bank or building society account.

For claims to Employment and Support Allowance or Universal Credit

Send the fee form to the nearest Jobcentre Plus office.

Send the fee form to the relevant benefit address.
